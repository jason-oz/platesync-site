# PlateSync — Product Discovery

## The User

**Primary:** Parents in households with 2+ people eating together regularly. One person (usually mum or dad) is responsible for planning, shopping, and cooking most meals. They may be tracking their own protein/macros, managing a partner's dietary goals, and navigating at least one child's allergies, preferences, or aversions.

**Secondary:** Health-conscious adults sharing a household (couples, housemates) where each person has different calorie or macro targets — e.g., one person training, one person not.

**Not the user:** Individual meal preppers, people cooking only for themselves, or households with no dietary differentiation between members.

## The Job

Stop guessing what to cook, how much to buy, and how much to plate for each person — every week.

The job is not "eat healthier" or "track macros." It's the weekly logistics problem: what are we eating, do we have everything, and who gets how much?

## The Core Flow

1. **Family setup** — Add household members with age, weight, activity level, goals, allergies, and food preferences. One-time setup, updated as things change.
2. **Recipe collection** — Import or build a recipe library. AI handles ingredient cataloguing and nutrition.
3. **Weekly plan** — Assign meals to days. App ranks by pantry stock and flags allergy conflicts.
4. **Shopping list** — Confirm plan → full-batch shopping list with pantry deducted.
5. **Cook** — Open meal on phone → step-by-step cooking guide with per-person portion breakdown.
6. **Leftovers** — Mark as cooked, log what's left (fridge/freeze/discard). Leftovers surface for future meals.

## Success Metrics

- **Primary:** User plans ≥3 meals in their first week (activation signal)
- **Secondary:** User returns to plan again the following week (retention signal)
- **Qualitative:** "I didn't have to think about portions" — the maths is invisible
- **Lagging:** Household invites a second member (signals real household adoption, not solo use)

## Non-Goals

- This is not a nutrition coaching app — no advice, no recommendations, no "you should eat less"
- This is not a recipe discovery platform — the recipe library is a means, not the product
- This is not a fitness tracker — training schedules feed portion calculation, not workout logging
- This is not designed for restaurants, meal services, or B2B food businesses
- No social features, public profiles, or recipe sharing between households

## Key Differentiator

Every other meal planner calculates one plan for one person (or one generic household). PlateSync calculates a different portion for every person at the table, adjusted per day based on their training schedule. That specificity is the product.
