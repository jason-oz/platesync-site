# PlateSync — FAQ (Sales)

## How does it calculate portions?

It uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation to calculate each person's daily calorie needs based on their age, weight, height, sex, and activity level. Protein targets use current sports nutrition research (0.9–2.2g per kg of body weight depending on age, activity, and goals). Portions are then capped so the total never exceeds what the recipe actually makes — if a recipe serves 4, the family gets 4 servings distributed by need, not 5.3.

## Do targets change throughout the week?

Yes. If a family member has a weekly training schedule set up (e.g., training Mon/Wed/Fri, rest the other days), their calorie and protein targets adjust per day — higher on training days, lower on rest days. Portion tendency (smaller/normal/larger eater) also factors in.

## Does it work for kids?

Yes. You can set up child profiles with age-appropriate goals ("growing", "active growing"), fussy eater notes, texture aversions, preferred foods, and disliked foods. Recipes flag allergen conflicts automatically. Children get appropriately smaller portions based on their age and weight — a toddler might get 0.5 servings while an adult gets 1.5.

## What happens to leftovers?

PlateSync assumes you cook the full recipe. After marking a meal as cooked, you're shown how many surplus servings are left and can choose to: keep them in the fridge (4-day expiry), freeze them (no expiry), or discard them. Leftovers show up as an option when planning future meals — no extra shopping needed.

## How does the shopping list work?

When you confirm a meal plan, it aggregates full-batch ingredient amounts for every fresh-cook recipe (because you cook the whole thing, not a fraction). It subtracts what you already have in your pantry, then AI normalises quantities to practical shopping amounts (e.g., "375g flour" becomes "500g flour" because that's what you can actually buy).

## Can I undo a meal if I didn't cook it?

Yes. In cooking mode there's a "Didn't Cook" option that returns all the full-batch ingredients back to your pantry — reversing the deduction that happened when you confirmed the plan.

## Can multiple people use it?

Yes. The household owner invites members by email from Settings. Everyone in the household shares the same recipes, pantry, meal plans, and family member profiles. Each person logs in with their own account.

## What cuisines and diets does it support?

Cuisines: Mediterranean, Asian, Indian, Mexican, Japanese, Korean, Thai, Middle Eastern, American, and African. Diets: balanced, high protein, Mediterranean, keto, paleo, Whole30, vegetarian, and vegan. You can combine a cuisine with a diet (e.g., Asian + keto). Each family member can also have their own diet override.

## Is my data private?

Yes. All household data is secured with row-level security — only members of your household can see your recipes, pantry, and meal plans. Different households cannot access each other's data. No personal health data is ever sent to AI providers.
